Biography

I am a PhD student in Linguistics at the University of Georgia. My research lies at the intersection of theoretical linguistics and neurolinguistics with a special focus on psycholinguistics and aphasia. I work with the Aphasia and Related Conditions (ARC) Lab and Building Rehabilitation Advances in Neurosciences (BRAIN) Lab, and I co‑lead the Second Language Acquisition and Cognition (SLAC) Lab efforts in methodological training and study design.

My projects examine how working memory and verb argument structure influence real‑time sentence processing. I use eye‑tracking to study processing of syntactic complexity such as relative clauses and island structures, while carefully controlling lexical frequency and visualizability of stimuli.
